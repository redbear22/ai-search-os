## The Core Principle

**Widen the moat. Don't chase parity.**

Watch the market closely — then mostly build your own thing harder. Reacting to every competitor move pulls toward parity and away from what makes the product distinct. The best version of this tool wins a category it defines, not one it joins late.

---

## Market Reality (verified June 2026)

- **Citation monitoring is crowded and commoditised.** Profound (G2 Winter 2026 AEO Leader), CitationLab, SE Visible, Otterly.AI (from $29/mo), and dozens of others launched across 2024–2025.
- **Real-time alerting is now a baseline feature**, not a differentiator — it's one of the three things every leading monitoring tool already has.
- **CitationLab is real** (citationlab.ai — real-time tracking across ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity, presence %, citation share, competitor tracking, drop alerts). It is *one of many*, not a singular threat.

**Implication:** Matching real-time monitoring drops aisearchrank into the most crowded corner of the market — a race to parity against funded incumbents. That is the wrong direction.

## The True Differentiator

The monitoring crowd tells you **where you stand**.  
aisearchrank tells you where you stand **and fixes it — autonomously.**

**Category: Autonomous GEO/AEO remediation.** Not monitoring. Remediation.

What's genuinely rare (verified against the competitive field):
- Zero-click visibility tracking
- AI comparison across 4+ models (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini)
- Agency white-label
- **Audit → Gaps → Action Plan → autonomous Fix** workflow

No competitor in the 2026 field is doing agent-driven *fixing*. That's the moat.

## Roadmap — In Priority Order

### 1. Finish Phase 1 (don't let market intel derail it)
Close the mid-flight kill test → wire the UI → audit-from-dashboard lands in Postgres. This is the differentiator going live. **Ship it before reacting to anything in the market.**

### 2. Phase 2 — Fix Agent (the moat-widener)
Autonomous, HITL-gated remediation. The thing the entire monitoring market structurally *cannot* answer. Already half-scaffolded in the fixes pipeline. **This is worth more than matching any competitor's monitoring.**

### 3. Surface what already exists (cheapest revenue)
- Promote the zero-click dashboard (already built)
- Move entity-trust / entity-footprint scoring from Admin → main UI
- Near-zero engineering, real market value

### 4. Real-time monitoring — last, and on your terms
Only to round out the suite for completeness — **not** as a defensive reaction. By the time you add it, it's a checkbox, not a scramble.

## Data Hygiene Rule

Market stats go stale and get repeated loosely. Before any number enters marketing or a roadmap decision, **verify it.**

Verified corrections so far:
- ❌ "60% zero-click" → ✅ **68%** (SparkToro/Similarweb, June 2026; 60% was the *2024* threshold). Sharper framing: **only 276 of every 1,000 searches reach the open web.**
- ⚠️ Methodology caveat: year-over-year deltas use different panels/providers — trend is solid, precise jumps are softer. Lead with direction + current number.

**Still unverified (do NOT use until checked):**
- 8.2 average citations in Perplexity
- 86% brand-controllable citations
- Entity footprints = 3–5x more citations
- CitationLab's specific "June 8" launch date (product is real; date unconfirmed)

## The Trap to Avoid

"Keep up with the marketplace" is the right instinct with one failure mode: competitor-reactive building drifts you toward parity and erodes what's distinct. The discipline is to watch the market, verify what's real, and then build your *own* thing deeper. Win the category you define.
